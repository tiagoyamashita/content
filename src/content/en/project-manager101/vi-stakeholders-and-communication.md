Stakeholders and communication
Project communication exists to create understanding, decisions, and action. Sending more updates does not fix unclear ownership or governance.

## Map stakeholders

Identify people affected by the project or able to affect it. Understand their goals, concerns, authority, influence, preferred detail, and required decisions.

| Group | Typical need |
|-------|--------------|
| Sponsor | Outcomes, major risks, trade-offs, and decisions |
| Delivery team | Priorities, dependencies, decisions, and near-term plan |
| Functional leaders | Capacity, cross-team impacts, and escalation |
| Users or operators | Readiness, training, support, and feedback channels |
| Governance bodies | Evidence, compliance, budget, and formal approvals |

Avoid labeling stakeholders as “resistant” before understanding incentives and consequences for them.

## Define governance

Document:

- who approves scope, budget, dates, and acceptance;
- which decisions the team can make independently;
- escalation thresholds and response times;
- meeting cadence and required artifacts;
- where decisions and current plans are recorded.

Use a RACI matrix sparingly for important deliverables: responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed. Each item should have one accountable owner.

## Write a useful status report

Keep it brief:

```text
Overall status and forecast
Outcomes or milestones completed
Next milestones and dates
Top risks, issues, and dependencies
Decisions needed, owner, and deadline
Changes since the previous report
```

Use red, amber, and green only with defined thresholds. Green should not mean “the team is working hard.”

## Run decision-oriented meetings

Send context and required decisions in advance. During the meeting, separate discussion from decision, name the decision owner, and record the result, rationale, owner, and due date. Cancel recurring meetings that have no current purpose.

## Difficult updates

Lead with the changed forecast and impact. Explain causes without blame, acknowledge uncertainty, and offer realistic options. Do not delay bad news to make the report look better; delay usually reduces the available choices.

## Communication plan

For each audience, define message, purpose, format, owner, cadence, and feedback channel. Review the plan as stakeholders and project phases change.
