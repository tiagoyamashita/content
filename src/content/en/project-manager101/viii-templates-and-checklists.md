Templates and checklists
Keep project artifacts as small as the decision requires. These templates are starting points, not mandatory bureaucracy.

## One-page project brief

```text
Project:
Sponsor:
Project manager:

Problem:
Desired outcome:
Success measures:

In scope:
Out of scope:
Key deliverables and acceptance:

Target dates and constraints:
Major assumptions and risks:
Key stakeholders and decision rights:
Approval to proceed:
```

## RAID entry

```text
ID and type: risk | assumption | issue | dependency
Description:
Impact:
Probability (for risks):
Owner:
Response or next action:
Action owner and due date:
Trigger or escalation threshold:
Status and last update:
```

## Weekly status

```text
Overall status: green | amber | red
Forecast:
Completed outcomes:
Next milestones:
Top risks/issues/dependencies:
Decisions needed, owners, deadlines:
Approved changes:
```

## Decision log

```text
Date:
Decision:
Decision owner:
Options considered:
Rationale and evidence:
Consequences:
Follow-up owner and date:
```

## Change request

```text
Requested change and reason:
Outcome/user impact:
Scope, schedule, cost, quality, and risk impact:
Alternatives:
Recommendation:
Decision owner and latest decision date:
Decision:
```

## Closeout checklist

- Deliverables meet acceptance criteria.
- Sponsor or authorized reviewer accepted the result.
- Operational ownership and support path are documented.
- Remaining actions have owners and dates.
- Contracts, access, records, and budget are closed.
- Lessons and decisions are archived.
- Closure is communicated.
- Benefits review has an owner and date.

## Practice exercise

Choose a small four-week initiative. Write the project brief, build a milestone plan, identify five RAID entries, send a one-page status update, and conduct a closeout. Review which artifact changed a decision; simplify anything that did not help.
