Delivery and closure
Delivery is more than finishing implementation. The result must be accepted, usable, supportable, and owned after the temporary project team disbands.

## Monitor execution

Use a regular cadence to review:

- accepted deliverables and milestone forecast;
- work blocked by decisions or dependencies;
- top risks and active issues;
- capacity and budget variance;
- changes to assumptions or scope;
- readiness for the next handoff.

Focus attention on exceptions and trends. A dashboard should support a conversation, not replace one.

## Prepare for release or handover

Confirm the relevant items:

| Area | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| Acceptance | Criteria tested and approved |
| Operations | Owner, runbook, monitoring, and support path |
| Users | Communication, training, and accessible guidance |
| Security/compliance | Required reviews and records complete |
| Data | Migration, validation, retention, and rollback plan |
| Release | Cutover steps, owners, timing, and go/no-go authority |
| Recovery | Rollback or contingency tested where practical |

Readiness should be evidence-based. A meeting cannot make incomplete controls complete.

## Close the project

1. Obtain acceptance for deliverables.
2. Transfer ownership and remaining actions.
3. Close contracts and reconcile budget.
4. Archive the final plan, decisions, and essential records.
5. Release resources and close temporary access.
6. Capture lessons with the people who did the work.
7. Communicate closure and the ongoing support route.
8. Schedule a benefits review if success appears later.

## Retrospective

Ask what helped, what hindered, what surprised the team, and what should change next time. Convert the most valuable lessons into owned improvements. A long list without follow-through is only documentation.

## Benefits review

Delivery metrics answer whether the project produced its outputs. Benefits metrics answer whether those outputs created the intended outcome. Assign an operational or business owner to measure adoption, savings, quality, revenue, risk reduction, or another agreed result after closure.

## Premature closure warning

Do not declare success solely because the deadline arrived, the budget was spent, or software was deployed. Compare the accepted result with the objective and document any remaining gap.
