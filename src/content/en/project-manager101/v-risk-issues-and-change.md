Risk, issues, and change
Projects need a shared way to manage uncertainty and deviations. A RAID log keeps risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies visible, but the conversations and decisions matter more than the spreadsheet.

## Distinguish the records

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Risk | Uncertain future event | Vendor may miss the delivery date |
| Assumption | Belief used for planning | Existing data meets migration quality needs |
| Issue | Problem happening now | Test environment is unavailable |
| Dependency | Required input or relationship | Legal approval before customer rollout |

## Manage risks

Describe risks as cause → event → impact. Score probability and impact consistently, then prioritize exposure rather than debating false precision.

Choose a response:

- **Avoid:** change the plan to remove the threat.
- **Mitigate:** reduce probability or impact.
- **Transfer:** move financial or operational exposure through a contract or service.
- **Accept:** monitor it and define a contingency trigger.

Every material risk needs an owner. A mitigation also needs an action owner and due date.

## Resolve issues

Record severity, impact, owner, next action, target resolution, and escalation threshold. For urgent issues, establish a short decision cadence and keep a timestamped record of facts and actions.

## Control changes

A change request should explain:

1. the requested change and reason;
2. impact on outcome and users;
3. schedule, cost, quality, resource, and risk effects;
4. alternatives, including doing nothing;
5. recommendation and decision owner.

Small changes can use lightweight approval, while material baseline changes need sponsor or governance approval. After a decision, update the plan, acceptance criteria, and stakeholder communication together.

## Escalate effectively

Escalation is not failure. Escalate when a decision exceeds the team's authority, a threshold is crossed, or delay would remove options. Bring verified facts, impacts, options, and the latest useful decision time.

## Warning signs

- A risk log that is reviewed only before audits.
- “High” risks with no trigger or response.
- Scope additions described as quick favors.
- Issues repeatedly deferred without a decision owner.
- Plans updated without recording what changed and who approved it.
