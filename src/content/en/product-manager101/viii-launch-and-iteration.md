Launch and iteration
A launch coordinates product readiness, go-to-market, operations, and measurement. It begins a new learning phase; it does not prove the product outcome.

## Choose a rollout

| Approach | Use when |
|----------|----------|
| Internal or dogfood | Teams can find obvious workflow and operational problems |
| Private pilot | Close support and rapid learning are valuable |
| Percentage rollout | Risk should be limited while behavior is monitored |
| Market or segment rollout | Operational, legal, or positioning needs differ |
| General availability | Readiness and support can handle broad access |

Set entry, pause, rollback, and expansion criteria. Feature flags and staged communication can make high-risk releases more reversible.

## Launch readiness

Coordinate:

- target audience, positioning, and message;
- final scope, experience, quality, accessibility, and performance;
- security, privacy, legal, and compliance approval;
- pricing, packaging, billing, and entitlement behavior;
- documentation, training, support scripts, and escalation;
- analytics, dashboards, data validation, and experiment setup;
- infrastructure capacity, monitoring, rollout, and recovery;
- sales and customer-success enablement where relevant.

Assign one owner to each readiness item and one decision owner for go/no-go.

## After launch

Monitor:

1. safety, errors, support load, and severe customer impact;
2. reach and exposure to the new experience;
3. activation or task completion;
4. repeated use and retention over an appropriate window;
5. business impact and guardrails;
6. qualitative feedback and unexpected behavior.

Separate launch noise from durable effects. Some outcomes require weeks or months.

## Triage feedback

Classify feedback by user segment, job, severity, frequency, evidence, and strategic relevance. Do not convert every comment directly into backlog work. Investigate clusters and the underlying unmet need.

## Lifecycle decisions

Use evidence to:

- **scale** when value and operations are healthy;
- **iterate** when the opportunity remains sound but execution is weak;
- **hold** when more observation is required;
- **reposition** when a different segment or use case shows stronger fit;
- **sunset** when value, strategy, economics, or risk no longer justify support.

For retirement, plan migration, data access, contracts, communication, support, and a fair transition period.

## Launch review

Compare actual behavior with the pre-launch hypothesis. Record what was learned, which assumptions changed, and the next product decision. Avoid a retrospective that discusses only delivery mechanics while ignoring whether users received value.
