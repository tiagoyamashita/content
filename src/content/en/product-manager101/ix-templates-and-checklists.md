Templates and checklists
Use the smallest artifact that improves a decision. Product documents should preserve context and evidence, not create work for their own sake.

## Opportunity brief

```text
Target user and situation:
Problem or unmet need:
Observed behavior and consequences:
Evidence and sources:
Current alternatives:
User value if solved:
Business and strategic relevance:
Key assumptions:
Riskiest unknown and next learning step:
```


```text

Guardrails:

Result and decision:
```

## Launch checklist

- Target audience and positioning are aligned.
- Scope, quality, accessibility, and performance are ready.
- Security, privacy, legal, and operational reviews are complete.
- Support, documentation, and internal teams are prepared.
- Instrumentation and dashboards are validated.
- Rollout, pause, and recovery criteria have owners.
- Go/no-go decision owner is named.
- Post-launch review has a date and decision framework.

## Product decision record


## Practice exercise

Pick an existing product. Write an opportunity brief from observable evidence, define one outcome metric and two guardrails, compare three solution approaches, and design the smallest ethical test of the riskiest assumption.
