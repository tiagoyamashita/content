Potato gnocchi (nhoque)
Soft potato dumplings made from scratch — the Brazilian/Italian **nhoque** you boil and toss with sauce. Light dough needs **dry potatoes** and as little flour as will hold. **All weights are in grams** — zero a bowl on the scale and add each ingredient directly.

**Yield:** about 4 servings · **Active:** ~45 min · **Total:** ~1¼ h

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Pot for boiling potatoes and another for cooking gnocchi
- Potato ricer or food mill (preferred) or fork
- Bench scraper, fork for ridges (optional)
- Baking sheet, lightly floured

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Starchy potatoes (russet / baking type), whole | 800 |
| All-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting | 180–220 |
| Egg yolk | 20 |
| Fine salt | 6 |
| Fresh nutmeg, grated (optional) | 0.5 |
| Semolina or extra flour, for the tray | 20 |

**Potato note:** 800 g whole potatoes yield roughly **550–600 g** riced flesh after peeling. Weigh the **riced potato** and adjust flour — wetter potatoes need more flour; drier ones need less.

## Method

### 1. Cook and dry the potatoes

1. Scrub **800 g** potatoes. Boil in salted water until a knife slides through easily, **25–35 minutes** depending on size. Do not pierce repeatedly (they soak water).
2. Drain. While hot, peel. Rice or mash finely onto a tray. Spread out **5–10 minutes** so steam escapes — dry potato is the secret to light nhoque.

### 2. Make the dough

1. Weigh the riced potato (target ~**580 g**). Mound on a counter, make a well.
2. Add **20 g egg yolk**, **6 g salt**, and **0.5 g nutmeg**. Scatter **180 g flour** over the potato.
3. Cut and fold with a bench scraper, then gently knead **30–60 seconds** until it just comes together. Add flour only until it is soft, slightly tacky, and holds a rope — **do not knead like bread**.
4. If the dough smears or sticks badly, dust with flour up to **220 g** total. Stop as soon as it is workable.

### 3. Shape

1. Divide into **4** pieces. Roll each into a rope ~**2 cm** thick on a lightly floured surface.
2. Cut into **2 cm** pillows (~**8–10 g** each). Optional: roll each piece over a fork or gnocchi board for ridges (helps sauce cling).
3. Set on a floured or semolina-dusted tray in a single layer.

### 4. Cook

1. Bring a wide pot of water to a boil. Season with salt (**~10 g per liter**).
2. Cook gnocchi in batches so they are not crowded. Stir gently once.
3. When they **float**, cook **30–45 seconds** more, then lift out with a slotted spoon.
4. Toss immediately with warm sauce — [simple tomato](../sauces/i-simple-tomato-sauce.md), [brown butter sage](../sauces/ii-brown-butter-sage.md), or [basil pesto](../sauces/iii-basil-pesto.md).

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Boil 800 g potatoes → peel → rice → cool/dry → weigh flesh (~580 g)
Counter → potato → 20 g yolk → 6 g salt → 0.5 g nutmeg → 180–220 g flour
Fold gently → ropes → cut → tray → boil until they float → sauce
```

## Make-ahead

| Stage | How |
|-------|-----|
| **Shaped, uncooked** | Freeze on a tray, then bag up to **1 month**; boil from frozen, add ~**1 minute** |
| **Cooked** | Best fresh; leftovers fridge **1 day**, reheat gently in sauce |

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **No egg** | Skip yolk; start with **160 g** flour and add as needed (more fragile) |
| **Richer** | Add **20 g** grated Parmesan to the dough |
| **Sweet potato** | Same method; expect a softer dough and slightly more flour |
| **Pan-fried finish** | After boiling, sear in butter until golden, then sauce |

## Serving ideas

- Tomato sauce + Parmesan
- Brown butter, sage, and black pepper
- Pesto and a spoon of pasta water
- Leftover [Lasagna Bolognese](i-lasagna-bolognese.md) ragù thinned with a splash of water

## Storage

Freeze uncooked gnocchi as above. Cooked leftovers: airtight in the fridge **1 day**.
