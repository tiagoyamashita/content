Lasagna Bolognese
Layered pasta with **ragù Bolognese**, **béchamel**, and a little Parmesan. Closer to the Emilia-Romagna style (meat sauce + white sauce) than a heavy ricotta–tomato American tray. **All weights are in grams** — zero a bowl on the scale and add each ingredient directly.

**Yield:** one 23 × 33 cm (9 × 13 in) pan, about 8 servings · **Oven:** 180 °C (350 °F) · **Active:** ~1 h · **Total:** ~3–3½ h (sauce simmer included)

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Wide heavy pot or Dutch oven (ragù)
- Medium saucepan (béchamel)
- Whisk, wooden spoon
- 23 × 33 cm baking dish
- Ladle, foil

## Ingredients

### Ragù Bolognese

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Yellow onion, finely diced | 120 |
| Carrot, finely diced | 80 |
| Celery, finely diced | 60 |
| Olive oil | 20 |
| Unsalted butter | 20 |
| Ground beef (about 80% lean) | 400 |
| Ground pork | 200 |
| Dry white wine | 120 |
| Whole milk | 150 |
| Crushed tomatoes or passata | 400 |
| Fine salt | 8 |
| Black pepper | 1 |
| Fresh nutmeg, grated (pinch) | 0.5 |
| Bay leaf | 1 leaf |

Optional but traditional: **50 g** diced pancetta with the soffritto; **100 g** chicken liver, minced, with the meat (richer sauce).

### Béchamel

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Unsalted butter | 60 |
| All-purpose flour | 60 |
| Whole milk, warm | 700 |
| Fine salt | 4 |
| Fresh nutmeg, grated | 1 |
| Black pepper | 0.5 |

### Assembly

| Ingredient | Weight (g) / amount |
|------------|---------------------|
| Dried lasagna sheets (no-boil or boil-first — see note) | 250–300 |
| Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated | 120 |
| Unsalted butter, small dots for the top | 15 |

**Pasta note:** If sheets must be boiled, cook in salted water until flexible but still firm, drain, and lay flat on a lightly oiled tray so they don’t stick. No-boil sheets: use as directed on the box and keep sauces a little looser.

## Method

### 1. Ragù

1. **Soffritto:** In the Dutch oven over medium heat, warm **20 g olive oil** and **20 g butter**. Soften **120 g onion**, **80 g carrot**, and **60 g celery** until translucent and sweet, **10–12 minutes** — do not brown hard.
2. **Meat:** Raise heat to medium-high. Add **400 g beef** and **200 g pork**. Break up and cook until no longer pink and most liquid has cooked off, **8–10 minutes**.
3. **Wine:** Pour in **120 g white wine**. Scrape the pot. Simmer until the sharp alcohol smell is gone, **3–5 minutes**.
4. **Milk:** Stir in **150 g milk**. Simmer gently until mostly absorbed, **5–8 minutes** (helps tenderize and mellow the meat).
5. **Tomato:** Add **400 g crushed tomatoes**, **8 g salt**, **1 g pepper**, **0.5 g nutmeg**, and the bay leaf. Bring to a bubble, then low simmer, partly covered, **1½–2 hours**. Stir every 15–20 minutes. Sauce should be thick, glossy, and spoonable — not watery. Adjust salt. Remove bay leaf. Cool slightly.

### 2. Béchamel

1. Melt **60 g butter** in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in **60 g flour**. Cook **1–2 minutes** (pale blond — not brown).
2. Whisk in **700 g warm milk** in a few pours until smooth.
3. Simmer, whisking, until it coats a spoon, **5–8 minutes**. Season with **4 g salt**, **1 g nutmeg**, **0.5 g pepper**. Keep warm and pourable (thin with a splash of milk if it sets thick).

### 3. Assemble

1. Heat oven to **180 °C**. Butter the baking dish lightly.
2. **Base:** Thin layer of ragù (or a spoon of béchamel) so pasta doesn’t stick.
3. **Layers** (repeat **4–5 times**):
   - Pasta sheets (trim to fit; slight overlap OK)
   - Ragù
   - Béchamel
   - Handful of the **120 g Parmesan** (save ~**30 g** for the top)
4. **Top:** Last pasta optional; finish with béchamel, remaining Parmesan, and **15 g** butter dots.
5. **Bake:** Tent loosely with foil. Bake **25 minutes**, uncover, bake **20–25 minutes** more until bubbling and golden at the edges.
6. **Rest:** **20–30 minutes** before cutting — slices hold together.

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Ragù pot: tare → 20 g oil → 20 g butter → 120 g onion → 80 g carrot → 60 g celery
  → 400 g beef → 200 g pork → 120 g wine → 150 g milk → 400 g tomato → season
Béchamel pan: tare → 60 g butter → 60 g flour → 700 g milk → season
Assembly: dish → sauces + 250–300 g pasta + 120 g Parmesan
```

## Make-ahead

| Stage | How |
|-------|-----|
| **Ragù** | Fridge **3 days** or freeze **3 months**; reheat before assembling |
| **Assembled unbaked** | Fridge **24 h**; add **10–15 minutes** bake time from cold |
| **Baked** | Fridge **3 days**; reheat covered at **160 °C** until hot |

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Beef only** | **600 g** beef, skip pork |
| **No wine** | Extra **80 g** milk or stock; longer simmer |
| **Greener sauce** | Stir **50 g** chopped parsley into ragù off heat |
| **Extra cheese** | Add **80 g** low-moisture mozzarella between middle layers (less traditional) |

## Storage

Refrigerate leftovers covered **3 days**. Freeze portions up to **2 months**; thaw overnight in the fridge, reheat covered until steaming.
