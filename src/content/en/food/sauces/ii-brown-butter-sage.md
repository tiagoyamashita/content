Brown butter sage
Nutty browned butter with crisp sage — the classic partner for [potato gnocchi (nhoque)](../savory/ii-potato-gnocchi.md) or cheese ravioli. Fast, and the timing is everything. **All weights are in grams**.

**Yield:** about 4 servings of gnocchi · **Total:** ~8–10 min

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Wide skillet
- Spatula or spoon

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Unsalted butter | 80 |
| Fresh sage leaves | 8–10 leaves (~3 g) |
| Fine salt | 2 |
| Black pepper | 1 |
| Lemon juice (optional, to finish) | 5 |
| Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated (to serve) | 40 |

## Method

1. Melt **80 g butter** in a wide skillet over medium heat.
2. When it foams, add the sage leaves. Swirl often.
3. Cook until the milk solids turn **hazelnut brown** and smell nutty, **3–5 minutes**. Pull from the heat as soon as the color is right — it goes from brown to burnt quickly.
4. Season with **2 g salt** and **1 g pepper**. Optional: **5 g lemon juice** to brighten.
5. Add drained hot gnocchi or pasta to the pan. Toss to coat. Serve with **40 g** Parmesan.

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Skillet → 80 g butter → melt → sage → brown to hazelnut
→ 2 g salt → 1 g pepper → (± 5 g lemon) → toss with gnocchi → Parmesan
```

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Walnut** | Add **40 g** toasted chopped walnuts with the sage |
| **Garlic** | Add **1** smashed clove for the last minute; discard before serving |
| **Brown butter only** | Skip sage; finish with parsley |
| **Richer** | Mount with **20 g** cold butter off heat for a glossier sauce |

## Storage

Best made fresh. Leftover brown butter (without sage) keeps in the fridge **1 week**; rewarm gently — solids may settle.
