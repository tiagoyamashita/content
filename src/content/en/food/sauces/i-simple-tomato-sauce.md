Simple tomato sauce
A weeknight **sugo** for pasta, [potato gnocchi (nhoque)](../savory/ii-potato-gnocchi.md), or as a pizza base. Bright tomato, garlic, and olive oil — no long simmer required. **All weights are in grams**.

**Yield:** about 4 pasta servings · **Active:** ~10 min · **Total:** ~25–30 min

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Medium saucepan
- Wooden spoon

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Crushed tomatoes or passata | 400 |
| Olive oil | 30 |
| Garlic, thinly sliced | 12 |
| Fine salt | 5 |
| Black pepper | 1 |
| Fresh basil leaves (optional) | 10 |
| Pinch of sugar (only if tomatoes taste sharp) | 2 |

Optional: **2 g** dried oregano, or **20 g** unsalted butter stirred in off heat for a rounder finish.

## Method

1. Warm **30 g olive oil** in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Soften **12 g garlic** until fragrant, **1–2 minutes** — do not brown.
2. Add **400 g tomatoes**, **5 g salt**, and **1 g pepper**. Bring to a gentle simmer.
3. Cook uncovered, stirring often, **15–20 minutes** until slightly thickened and less raw-tasting. Adjust salt. Add **2 g sugar** only if needed.
4. Tear in **10 g basil** off heat. Toss with hot pasta or gnocchi; loosen with a spoon of cooking water if it looks tight.

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Pan → 30 g oil → 12 g garlic → soften
→ 400 g tomato → 5 g salt → 1 g pepper → simmer 15–20 min → basil
```

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Arrabbiata** | Add **3–5 g** chili flakes with the garlic |
| **Onion base** | Soften **80 g** diced onion before the garlic |
| **Meatier** | Brown **200 g** sausage or ground beef first, then continue |
| **Smooth** | Blend after cooking; return to the pan to reheat |

## Storage

Fridge **4 days**. Freeze up to **3 months**; thaw overnight and reheat gently.
