Basil pesto
Classic Genovese-style pesto: basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan, and olive oil. No cooking — just blending. **All weights are in grams**.

**Yield:** about 200 g sauce (4 pasta servings) · **Total:** ~10 min

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Food processor or mortar and pestle
- Spatula

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Fresh basil leaves, packed loosely then weighed | 50 |
| Pine nuts | 30 |
| Garlic | 8 |
| Parmigiano-Reggiano, finely grated | 40 |
| Pecorino Romano, finely grated (optional) | 20 |
| Extra-virgin olive oil | 80 |
| Fine salt | 3 |
| Lemon juice (optional) | 5 |

**Basil note:** Use only leaves (no thick stems). If basil is scarce, replace up to **15 g** with baby spinach — flavor will be milder.

## Method

1. Toast **30 g pine nuts** in a dry pan over medium heat until fragrant, **2–3 minutes**. Cool.
2. In a processor: pulse **8 g garlic**, toasted pine nuts, and **3 g salt**.
3. Add **50 g basil**. Pulse to a rough paste, scraping the bowl.
4. Add **40 g Parmesan** and **20 g Pecorino** (or **60 g** Parmesan total). Pulse briefly.
5. With the machine running, stream in **80 g olive oil** until saucy but not oily soup. Taste; add **5 g lemon** if you want brightness.
6. Toss with hot pasta or gnocchi using a spoon of cooking water so it emulsifies and coats.

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Toast 30 g pine nuts → cool
Processor → 8 g garlic → pine nuts → 3 g salt → pulse
→ 50 g basil → pulse → 40 g Parmesan → 20 g Pecorino
→ stream 80 g oil → (± 5 g lemon) → toss with pasta water
```

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Walnut pesto** | Replace pine nuts with **30 g** walnuts |
| **Almond** | **30 g** blanched almonds instead of pine nuts |
| **Dairy-free** | Skip cheese; add **15 g** nutritional yeast and extra salt to taste |
| **Parsley** | Half basil, half parsley for a cheaper, greener sauce |

## Storage

Fridge under a thin oil film **5 days**. Freeze in ice-cube trays up to **3 months**; thaw in the fridge.
