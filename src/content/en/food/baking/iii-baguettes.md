Baguettes
Crisp crust, open crumb, and a mild wheat flavor from a long cool rise. **All weights are in grams** — zero a bowl on the scale and add each ingredient directly. No mixer required.

**Yield:** 3 baguettes · **Oven:** 240 °C (465 °F) with steam · **Active:** ~45 min · **Total:** ~5–6 h (or overnight fridge rest)

## Equipment

- Kitchen scale (grams)
- Large mixing bowl
- Bench scraper, kitchen towel
- Baking sheet or baguette pan
- Parchment
- Sharp lame, razor, or serrated knife
- Spray bottle or oven-safe pan for steam

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Weight (g) |
|------------|------------|
| Bread flour (or strong all-purpose) | 500 |
| Water, room temperature | 360 |
| Fine salt | 10 |
| Instant yeast | 3 |

**Hydration note:** 360 g water in 500 g flour is **72%** — sticky at first, then smoother after folds. If your kitchen is very dry, keep **10 g** water back and add only if the dough feels stiff.

## Method

### 1. Mix and autolyse

1. In a large bowl, combine **500 g flour** and **360 g water**. Mix until no dry flour remains (shaggy is fine). Cover **20–30 minutes**.
2. Sprinkle **10 g salt** and **3 g instant yeast** over the dough. Pinch and fold until evenly distributed, **2–3 minutes**.

### 2. Bulk fermentation (with folds)

1. Cover and rest at room temperature (**22–24 °C** if possible).
2. Every **30 minutes** for the first **1½ hours**, do a set of **stretch-and-folds**: wet a hand, grab one side, stretch up, fold over; rotate the bowl and repeat 4 times.
3. After the folds, leave undisturbed until the dough is airy and roughly **doubled**, about **2–2½ hours** total from mixing (cooler rooms take longer).

**Overnight option:** After the last fold, refrigerate covered **8–16 hours**. Shape cold or after **30–45 minutes** at room temperature.

### 3. Divide and pre-shape

1. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured counter. Divide into **3** pieces (~**290 g** each).
2. Gently shape each into a loose cylinder. Rest covered **15–20 minutes**.

### 4. Final shape

1. Pat a piece into a rectangle (~**20 × 12 cm**). Fold the long top third down, seal with your fingertips. Fold the bottom third up, seal.
2. Roll from the center outward into a **35–40 cm** baguette with tapered ends. Seam side down.
3. Place on parchment (or a floured couche / towel with folds between loaves). Cover **45–60 minutes** until a finger poke springs back slowly.

### 5. Bake with steam

1. Heat oven to **240 °C** with a baking sheet (or stone) inside. Put an empty metal pan on a lower rack for steam.
2. Score each loaf with **3–4** overlapping diagonal cuts, **0.5–1 cm** deep.
3. Slide loaves onto the hot sheet. Pour **200 g** hot water into the steam pan (or mist the oven walls). Close the door quickly.
4. Bake **20–25 minutes** until deep golden. For a thicker crust, vent the door for the last **5 minutes**.
5. Cool on a rack **30–40 minutes** before slicing.

## Scale workflow (tare between steps)

```text
Bowl → tare → 500 g flour → 360 g water → mix → rest 20–30 min
→ 10 g salt → 3 g yeast → fold in
Bulk: folds every 30 min × 3 → finish rise
Divide ~290 g × 3 → pre-shape → rest → final shape → proof → score → bake
```

## Variations

| Change | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Softer crumb** | Water **380 g** (76%); expect stickier dough |
| **Whole wheat** | Replace **100 g** bread flour with whole wheat; add **15–20 g** water |
| **Sesame** | Brush with water and roll tops in seeds before the final proof |
| **Shorter day bake** | Skip fridge; use **4 g** yeast and watch the bulk rise carefully |

## Serving

Slice for sandwiches, serve with [garlic herb butter](../sauces/iv-garlic-herb-butter.md), soup, or cheese. Day-old baguettes toast well or become croutons.

## Storage

Best the day of baking. Room temperature in a paper bag **1 day**. Freeze whole or sliced up to **1 month**; refresh from frozen at **180 °C** for **8–10 minutes**.
