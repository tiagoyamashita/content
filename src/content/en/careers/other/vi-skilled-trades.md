Skilled trades
Construction, manufacturing, machinery, agriculture, food processing, logistics, and facilities work. Japan uses dedicated visa programs to address labor shortages in many of these fields.

Not legal advice. Safety and licensing rules are strict — verify with employers and the relevant authorities.

Parent: [Other careers](i-overview.md).

## Day-to-day

| Field | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Construction | Building, finishing, equipment operation, site support |
| Manufacturing / machinery | Assembly, machining, inspection, maintenance |
| Food & agriculture | Food processing, farming, packaging |
| Logistics / warehouse | Picking, sorting, forklift, distribution |
| Facilities / maintenance | Building systems, cleaning, repairs |

## Skills that matter

| Skill | Level | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Safety awareness | Core | Non-negotiable on sites and in plants |
| Manual skill & reliability | Core | Precision, consistency, punctuality |
| Japanese (practical) | Core | Safety instructions, teamwork, signage |
| Physical stamina | Core | Many roles are physically demanding |
| Equipment / machine operation | Core | Field-specific tools and machinery |
| Licenses & certifications | Stretch | Forklift, crane, welding, etc. raise pay |
| Team leadership | Stretch | Path to foreman / supervisor |

## Japan notes

- **Labor shortages** in construction, manufacturing, agriculture, and food processing drive active foreign recruitment.
- **Practical Japanese** for safety and teamwork is expected even where the work is hands-on.
- **Field-specific licenses** (e.g. forklift, welding, crane operation) increase pay and mobility.
- Some roles include **housing or dormitories**, especially outside major cities.

## Entry & qualifications

| Route | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Specified Skilled Worker (SSW) | Covers construction, manufacturing, agriculture, food, and more |
| Skilled Labor visa | For certain specialized, experienced trades |
| Technical training programs | Structured entry with employer support and progression |

Skills and Japanese-language tests are typically required; confirm the current field-specific pathway.

## Compensation (illustrative)

| Level | Rough ¥M / year |
|-------|-----------------|
| Entry / trainee | 2.6–3.6 |
| Skilled worker | 3.2–4.8 |
| Certified / specialized | 4–6 |
| Foreman / supervisor | 5–8+ |

Overtime, hazard/night allowances, licenses, and region affect pay. Certifications are the clearest way to raise the ceiling.

## How to get in / progress

1. Enter through SSW or a technical training program with a supporting employer.
2. Build practical Japanese for safety and teamwork.
3. Earn field licenses (forklift, welding, crane, etc.).
4. Progress to specialized, certified, and supervisory roles.
