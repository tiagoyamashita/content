Goals & metrics
Metrics without goals produce **dashboard theater**. Tie every channel to a **business outcome**, then pick a small set of **leading** indicators you can move weekly.

## 1. North-star metric

The **north star** is the single outcome that best reflects delivered customer value — often tied to revenue or retention.

| Business type | Example north star |
|---------------|-------------------|
| SaaS | Weekly active teams or net revenue retention |
| E-commerce | Orders or gross margin |
| Media / content | Engaged subscribers or ad-supported sessions |
| Marketplace | Successful transactions |

Marketing supports the north star; it is rarely identical to "traffic."

## 2. Leading vs lagging

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Leading** | Predict future results; you influence quickly | Email clicks, ranking impressions, trial starts |
| **Lagging** | Outcome after the fact | Revenue, churn, LTV |

Watch leading metrics in weekly reviews; lagging metrics in monthly/quarterly reviews.

## 3. Funnel metrics map

```text
Awareness      →  impressions, reach, branded search volume
Consideration  →  time on site, pages per session, email signups
Conversion     →  trial starts, purchases, demo requests
Retention      →  activation rate, repeat purchase, NPS
```

Define **one primary conversion** per site (signup, purchase, contact) and measure everything against it — see [Analytics — conversions](../analytics/iv-conversions-and-attribution.md).

## 4. Unit economics (marketing lens)

| Metric | Formula | Use |
|--------|---------|-----|
| **CAC** | Marketing + sales spend ÷ new customers | Can you afford this channel? |
| **LTV** | Average revenue (or margin) per customer × lifespan | Is CAC payback acceptable? |
| **Payback period** | Months until LTV covers CAC | Cash-flow planning |
| **ROAS** | Revenue ÷ ad spend | Paid efficiency (e-commerce) |
| **CPA** | Spend ÷ conversions | Paid efficiency (leads) |

Rough benchmarks beat no math. If CAC > LTV, fix offer or channel before scaling spend.

## 5. Vanity vs useful

| Vanity (context only) | Useful (actionable) |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Raw traffic | Traffic to **money pages** |
| Social followers | Link clicks to site |
| Email list size | Click rate + revenue per send |
| Keyword rankings alone | Rankings × search volume × CTR to converting page |

## 6. Goal-setting template (90 days)

| Goal | Metric | Baseline | Target | Owner |
|------|--------|----------|--------|-------|
| Grow organic trials | Organic signup sessions | 120/mo | 200/mo | Content |
| Improve signup rate | Landing page conversion | 2.1% | 3.0% | Product + marketing |
| Launch nurture | Welcome series completion | — | 80% delivered | Email |

Limit to **3–5 goals** per quarter.

## 7. Reporting cadence

| Cadence | Review |
|---------|--------|
| **Weekly** | Leading metrics, experiments, blockers |
| **Monthly** | Channel performance vs targets, content published |
| **Quarterly** | CAC/LTV, strategy, channel mix |

Use [Analytics — reporting](../analytics/v-reporting-and-experiments.md) for dashboard layout.

- Name one vanity metric and its useful counterpart.
