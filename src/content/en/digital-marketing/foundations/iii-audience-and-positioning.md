Audience & positioning
Marketing fails when it speaks to **everyone**. Define **who** you serve, **what job** you do for them, and **why you** over alternatives — then every page, ad, and email gets sharper.

## 1. Ideal customer profile (ICP)

An **ICP** is the type of customer you build and market for — not a single person, but a segment with shared traits.

| B2B example | B2C example |
|-------------|-------------|
| Company size, industry, role | Age band, hobby, budget |
| "Engineering lead at 20–200 person SaaS" | "Home coffee enthusiasts spending $500+/yr" |

| Field | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **Who** | Job title or demographic? |
| **Pain** | What frustrates them today? |
| **Goal** | What outcome do they want? |
| **Trigger** | What event makes them search or buy now? |
| **Objection** | Why might they choose a competitor or do nothing? |

Write one paragraph ICP before writing homepage copy.

## 2. Jobs to be done (JTBD)

People "hire" products to **make progress**, not to own features.

```text
When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].
```

Example: *When I launch a side project, I want hosting that deploys from Git, so I can ship without managing servers.*

Features map to jobs; marketing should lead with **outcome**, not feature lists.

## 3. Positioning statement (template)

```text
For [ICP]
who [pain / job],
[Product] is a [category]
that [key benefit].
Unlike [alternative],
we [differentiator].
```

Keep it internal unless every word is defensible publicly. Derive **headlines**, **meta titles**, and **ad hooks** from it.

## 4. Competitive framing

| Frame | When to use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Category leader** | You are the default name in the space |
| **Specialist** | You win a niche the generalist ignores |
| **Challenger** | You attack incumbent on price, speed, or UX |
| **New category** | You redefine the problem (hard — needs education) |

**Comparison pages** ("Us vs X") work in consideration stage — be factual, not petty. Link from [Content strategy](../content-strategy/i-overview.md) pillar pages.

## 5. Messaging hierarchy

```text
Tagline (5 words)     →  memory
Value prop (1 sentence) →  homepage hero
Proof (3 bullets)     →  logos, metrics, testimonials
Detail (pages)        →  docs, blog, case studies
```

One message per page. Homepage ≠ pricing page ≠ blog post.

## 6. Voice and tone

| Voice (consistent) | Tone (varies by context) |
|--------------------|---------------------------|
| Expert vs friendly | Celebratory launch vs serious security post |
| Formal vs casual | Match buyer expectations in your industry |

Document 3–5 voice rules for anyone writing copy (including AI drafts).

## 7. Research without a big budget

| Method | Output |
|--------|--------|
| **5 customer interviews** | Pain quotes for landing pages |
| **Review mining** (G2, App Store, Reddit) | Language buyers use |
| **Search autocomplete / People Also Ask** | Questions to answer in content |
| **Sales/support tickets** | Objections and FAQ |

## 8. Rehearsal questions

- What is an ICP and why narrow beats broad?
- Write a one-sentence JTBD for a product you know.
- What belongs in a positioning statement vs on a feature page?
