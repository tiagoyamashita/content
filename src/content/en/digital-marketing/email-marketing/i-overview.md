Email marketing — overview
**Email** is an **owned** channel — you reach people who opted in without paying per send or fighting an algorithm. It nurtures consideration, drives conversions, and supports retention.

Transactional email (password resets) is separate from **marketing** email (newsletters, promos). Both need deliverability basics.

## 1. Why email still matters

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Owned audience** | List survives platform changes |
| **High intent** | They asked to hear from you |
| **Measurable** | Opens (directional), clicks, revenue per send |
| **Automation** | Lifecycle flows run 24/7 |

Pair with [Content strategy](../content-strategy/v-distribution-and-repurposing.md) — blog brings subscribers; email brings them back.

## 2. Email types

| Type | Trigger | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Transactional** | User action | Receipt, reset password |
| **Marketing** | Campaign schedule | Newsletter, product launch |
| **Lifecycle** | Automation rules | Welcome series, cart abandon |

Don't mix marketing into transactional streams — separate domains or subdomains if volume is high.

## 4. Metrics (healthy direction)

| Metric | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **List growth rate** | Quality > quantity |
| **Click rate** | More reliable than open rate (Apple MPP inflates opens) |
| **Unsubscribe rate** | Spike = wrong segment or fatigue |
| **Spam complaints** | Must stay tiny (<0.1%) |
| **Revenue per email** | E-commerce / paid products |
