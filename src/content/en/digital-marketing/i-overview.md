Digital marketing — overview
**Digital marketing** is how you attract, convert, and retain customers through online channels — search, content, email, paid ads, and social. This track focuses on **durable, compounding** tactics, not one-off hacks.

Assumes you can ship a website or product. Not legal or tax advice.

## Channels at a glance

| Channel | Primary job | Compounds? | Typical owner |
|---------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **Organic search (SEO)** | Capture intent when people look for answers | Yes — pages rank for years | Content + technical |
| **Content / blog** | Educate, rank, earn links and trust | Yes | Content |
| **Email** | Nurture and monetize people who opted in | Yes — list is an asset | Marketing / product |
| **Paid search / social** | Buy reach and clicks on demand | No — stops when spend stops | Growth |
| **Social (organic)** | Community, brand, distribution | Sometimes | Founder / community |
| **Referral / partnerships** | Borrow another audience's trust | Depends on deal | BD / founder |

SEO and content feed **link building**; analytics tells you whether any channel pays off. Paid works best when organic and on-page already prove **message–market fit** on a landing page.

## How the pieces fit

```text
Product / site
  → technical foundation (fast, crawlable, HTTPS)
  → on-page SEO (titles, structure, intent-matched content)
  → content strategy (pillars, calendar, link-worthy assets)
  → link building (other sites vouch for you with links)
  → measurement (rankings, traffic, conversions)
  → email + paid (scale and retain what works)
```

Link building does not replace good content or a working product — it **amplifies** pages that already deserve attention.

## Core vocabulary (basics)

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Impression** | Someone saw your listing or ad (not necessarily a click) |
| **CTR (click-through rate)** | Clicks ÷ impressions — title/meta and relevance |
| **Conversion** | Desired action completed (signup, purchase, demo) |
| **CAC (customer acquisition cost)** | Spend to acquire one customer |
| **LTV (lifetime value)** | Revenue (or margin) expected from one customer over time |
| **Organic vs paid** | Earned rankings/clicks vs bought placement |
| **Landing page** | URL you send traffic to — should match ad/query intent |
| **UTM parameters** | Tags on URLs so analytics attributes traffic to a campaign |

## Principles

| Principle | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **Earn links, don't buy them** | Paid link schemes violate search guidelines and risk penalties |
| **Match intent before scaling spend** | Ranking or ads on the wrong page waste clicks |
| **Relevance beats raw volume** | Ten links from niche authorities beat a thousand from spam directories |
| **Create link-worthy assets** | Guides, tools, data, and original research attract links naturally |
| **Measure conversions, not vanity** | Traffic without signups or revenue is a diagnostic, not a win |
| **Play the long game** | SEO, content, and email compound; paid is a throttle |

## When to invest in what

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| No site or unclear offer | [Foundations](foundations/iii-audience-and-positioning.md) — audience and positioning first |
| Site is new with thin content | [On-page SEO](on-page-seo/i-overview.md) + [Content strategy](content-strategy/i-overview.md) before outreach |
| Core pages exist and convert | [Link building](link-building/i-overview.md), [Email](email-marketing/i-overview.md), light [Paid](paid-advertising/i-overview.md) tests |
| Manual penalty or toxic links | Link audit — see [Measurement & pitfalls](link-building/v-measurement-and-pitfalls.md) |
| Local business | Local SEO (on-page + directories) + community partnerships |
| Proven landing page, need volume | [Paid](paid-advertising/i-overview.md) with strict CPA/ROAS caps and UTM hygiene |
