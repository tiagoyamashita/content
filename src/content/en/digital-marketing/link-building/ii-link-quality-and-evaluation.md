Link quality & evaluation
Not all links help equally. A small number of **relevant, editorial** links outperform hundreds of low-quality ones. Learn to evaluate prospects before you spend time on outreach.

## 1. The quality stack

Rank prospects (and audit existing links) on four dimensions:

```text
Relevance  →  Authority  →  Placement  →  Anchor
   (topic)      (trust)        (context)      (text)
```

| Dimension | Ask |
|-----------|-----|
| **Relevance** | Is this site in your niche or audience? |
| **Authority** | Is it a real brand with traffic and editorial standards? |
| **Placement** | Is the link in body content, or buried in footer/widget? |
| **Anchor** | Is the anchor natural (branded or descriptive), not over-optimized? |

A highly authoritative but **irrelevant** link (e.g. random news site linking to your dev tools blog in a paid footer) carries less value than a mid-authority **niche** link.

## 2. Authority signals (heuristic)

No public "Google score" exists. Use triangulation:

| Signal | How to check |
|--------|--------------|
| **Organic traffic** | SimilarWeb, Ahrefs, Semrush estimates |
| **Indexed pages** | `site:domain.com` in search |
| **Editorial quality** | Read 2–3 articles — human-written? |
| **Link profile** | Do they link out to quality sources, or only to ads? |
| **History** | Old domain with consistent topic vs expired domain repurposed for SEO |

Third-party metrics (**DR**, **DA**, **AS**) are **comparative shortcuts**, not ground truth. Use them to sort a prospect list, not as the only filter.

## 3. Placement matters

| Placement | Typical value |
|-----------|---------------|
| **In-content editorial** | Highest — surrounded by relevant text |
| **Author bio (guest post)** | Good — if site is real and post is substantive |
| **Resource / tools list** | Good — intent matches "best X tools" |
| **Sidebar / blogroll** | Lower — sitewide templates dilute per-link value |
| **Footer on unrelated pages** | Low — often paid or automated |
| **Comment / forum spam** | Harmful — ignore or disavow |

**Contextual** links (the sentence around the link explains why your page matters) beat naked URLs in link farms.

## 4. Anchor text profile

Search engines expect a **natural mix**:

| Anchor type | Example | Healthy share |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| **Branded** | `Acme`, `acme.com` | Often largest |
| **Naked URL** | `https://yoursite.com/guide` | Common |
| **Generic** | `here`, `this guide`, `learn more` | Common |
| **Partial match** | `link building guide` | Some |
| **Exact match** | `best link building software` | Small — overuse looks manipulative |

When requesting links, prefer **branded or descriptive** anchors over keyword stuffing. Let the **page title and content** carry keyword relevance.

## 5. Red flags (prospect or existing link)

| Red flag | Why it hurts |
|----------|--------------|
| Site exists only to sell links | Obvious manipulation |
| Unrelated topics on same domain (casino + SaaS + recipes) | PBN or expired-domain reuse |
| Thin AI content, no bylines | Low trust, link may disappear |
| "Write for us" pages with instant approval + fee | Paid network, not editorial |
| Thousands of outbound links per page | Link directory, not curation |
| Sudden DR spike on young domain | Often toxic acquisition |

**Rule of thumb:** Would you be proud to show this link to a customer? If not, skip it.

## 6. Competitor backlink analysis

Find who already links to competitors — they may link to you too.

```text
1. Pick 3–5 ranking URLs for your target keyword
2. Export referring domains (Ahrefs, Semrush, Moz)
3. Filter: dofollow, DR > 20, relevant category
4. Classify: guest post, roundup, resource page, news mention
5. Build outreach list from repeatable patterns
```

| Link type | Outreach angle |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Listicle** ("10 best X") | Pitch your product/content for next update |
| **Guest post** | Offer a complementary article (not duplicate topic) |
| **Resource page** | Suggest your guide fits their curated list |
| **Broken link** | Their page links to dead URL — offer your replacement |

## 7. Internal linking (related)

Backlinks are external; **internal links** distribute authority inside your site:

| Practice | Benefit |
|----------|---------|
| Link from high-traffic posts to **money pages** | Passes internal PageRank |
| Use descriptive anchor text | Clarifies target topic |
| Avoid orphan pages | Every important URL should be reachable in ≤3 clicks |
| Update old posts with links to new assets | Compounds without new backlinks |

Strong internal structure makes new backlinks **more effective** when they land on hub pages that pass equity downstream.

## 8. Scoring template (spreadsheet)

| Prospect URL | Relevance (1–5) | Authority (1–5) | Placement | Effort | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|--------|----------|
| example.com/review | 5 | 4 | In-content | Medium | **High** |
| random-dir.com | 2 | 3 | Footer | Low | Skip |

Sort by **priority = relevance × authority / effort**. Outreach the top tier first.
