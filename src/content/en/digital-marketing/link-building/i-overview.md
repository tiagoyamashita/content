Link building — overview
**Link building** is the practice of earning **hyperlinks from other websites** to yours. Search engines treat links as votes of trust; quality backlinks help pages rank and send **referral traffic** from audiences you do not already reach.

This submenu is practical: what links are, how to evaluate them, white-hat tactics, outreach, and what to avoid.

## 1. What is a backlink?

A **backlink** (inbound link) is a link on site A that points to a URL on site B.

```text
blog.example.com/article
  →  <a href="https://yoursite.com/guide">great guide</a>
       └── backlink to yoursite.com/guide
```

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Referring domain** | A unique site that links to you (one domain may link many times) |
| **Referring page** | The specific URL containing the link |
| **Target URL** | Your page receiving the link |
| **Anchor text** | The clickable words ("great guide") |

**Internal links** (your site → your site) help navigation and crawl paths but are not backlinks.

## 2. Why links matter

| Benefit | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Rankings** | Links are a major off-page signal; competitive queries are hard to win without them |
| **Discovery** | Crawlers follow links to find new pages |
| **Referral traffic** | Readers click — especially from high-traffic, relevant sites |
| **Brand** | Mentions on trusted publications build credibility |

Links are not the only ranking factor (content quality, technical SEO, user signals matter too), but for most commercial topics they remain **necessary** to compete.

## 3. Link attributes (basics)

| Attribute | Typical effect |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Dofollow** (default) | Passes ranking signals |
| **`rel="nofollow"`** | Hint not to pass credit; still can drive traffic |
| **`rel="sponsored"`** | Paid or compensated placement — disclose |
| **`rel="ugc"`** | User-generated content (comments, forums) |

Do not obsess over nofollow vs dofollow. A relevant **nofollow** link from a major publication can be more valuable than a dofollow link from a spam blog.

## 4. The link-building loop

```text
1. Asset     →  page worth linking to (guide, tool, study, template)
2. Prospect  →  sites that already link to similar content
3. Outreach  →  personalized pitch with clear value
4. Earn      →  editorial link, mention, or citation
5. Measure   →  rankings, referral traffic, new referring domains
```

Skip step 1 and outreach feels like spam. Skip step 5 and you cannot tell what works.

## 5. White hat vs black hat

| Approach | Examples | Risk |
|----------|----------|------|
| **White hat** | Original research, guest posts on real sites, digital PR, fixing broken links | Low — aligns with guidelines |
| **Gray hat** | Aggressive guest posting networks, excessive exact-match anchors | Medium — may work until it doesn't |
| **Black hat** | PBNs, paid link packages, automated comment spam, link farms | High — manual actions, deindexing |

This track covers **white hat** only. Short-term black-hat wins often end in **lost traffic** when algorithms or reviewers catch up.

## 6. Prerequisites before outreach

| Check | If missing |
|-------|------------|
| Target page is **published and useful** | Write or improve the asset first |
| Site is **crawlable** (no accidental `noindex`, robots blocks) | Fix technical SEO |
| **HTTPS** and reasonable page speed | See [Hosting & CDN](../../startups/free-services/iii-hosting-domains-and-cdn.md) |
| Clear **value proposition** in one sentence | Why should another site link? |

## 7. Quick vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Domain Rating (DR) / Domain Authority (DA)** | Third-party scores — useful for comparison, not Google metrics |
| **Link velocity** | Rate of new links — unnatural spikes look suspicious |
| **Anchor text profile** | Mix of branded, generic, and keyword anchors |
| **Toxic link** | Spammy or manipulative link that may harm trust |
