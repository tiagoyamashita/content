White-hat strategies
Sustainable links come from **real editorial decisions** — someone chose to cite your work because it helped their audience. These tactics align with that pattern.

## 1. Link-worthy assets (foundation)

Build pages others *want* to reference:

| Asset type | Why sites link | Example |
|------------|----------------|---------|
| **Definitive guide** | Saves research time | "Complete guide to X for Y audience" |
| **Original data / survey** | Journalists cite numbers | Annual industry benchmark report |
| **Free tool or calculator** | Utility + embed potential | ROI calculator, schema generator |
| **Template / checklist** | Practical download | Security audit checklist, Notion template |
| **Visual / infographic** | Embeddable with attribution | Process diagram with embed code |
| **Case study with metrics** | Proof for similar buyers | "How we cut latency 40%" |

```text
Weak asset   →  "About us" page, thin product blurb
Strong asset →  Solves a specific problem better than existing top results
```

Before outreach, search your target keyword. If your page is not **clearly better or different**, improve the asset first.

## 2. Digital PR & original research

**Digital PR** earns links from news and industry publications by giving them a **story hook**.

| Hook | Pitch angle |
|------|-------------|
| Survey data | "67% of developers X — new study" |
| Trend commentary | Expert quote on breaking news |
| Contrarian insight | Polite challenge to common wisdom (with evidence) |
| Local angle | City/regional data for local press |

**Workflow:**

```text
Hypothesis → collect data → publish on your site → press release / journalist list
         → offer exclusive to one outlet OR broad embargo
         → track pickups and unlinked mentions (request link)
```

Tools: HARO / Connectively (journalist requests), reverse image search for unlinked brand mentions.

## 3. Guest posting (done right)

Guest posting means writing a **genuine article** for another site's audience — not a 300-word advert with a link.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Pitch topics their readers need | Pitch the same post to 50 sites |
| Match their tone and depth | Stuff keyword anchors |
| Link once naturally to a **relevant** resource | Link to homepage only |
| Build relationship with editor | Pay "guest post" farms |

**Prospecting:** search `your topic + "guest post"`, `write for us`, or analyze where competitors guest-authored.

## 4. Resource page link building

Many sites maintain **curated lists** of tools, guides, or charities.

```text
Google:  "keyword" + "resources"
         "keyword" + "useful links"
         site:.edu "resources" + topic   (higher bar, high trust)
```

Outreach: explain **one sentence** why your page fits their list; suggest exact section if possible.

## 5. Broken link building

Find pages that link to **404 URLs**, offer your content as replacement.

```text
1. Find resource/list pages in your niche (Ahrefs "broken outbound links" or Check My Links extension)
2. Confirm the broken URL's original topic (Wayback Machine)
3. Publish or have a matching page on your site
4. Email: "I noticed link X is broken — we have an updated guide at Y"
```

Success rate is low but **conversion is high** — you are helping the webmaster fix their page.

## 6. Skyscraper technique (refined)

Classic skyscraper: find popular content → make something better → ask linkers to switch.

| Upgrade vector | Example |
|----------------|---------|
| **Freshness** | 2026 edition with new tools |
| **Depth** | 3× the examples, step-by-step screenshots |
| **Data** | Add benchmarks competitors lack |
| **UX** | Interactive version, printable PDF |

**Outreach tip:** do not say "mine is better" — say "I published an updated resource that covers [gap they care about]."

## 7. Partnerships & community

| Channel | Link opportunity |
|---------|------------------|
| **Integrations** | Partner directory, co-marketing blog |
| **Open source** | README, awesome lists, docs citations |
| **Sponsorships** | Event pages, nonprofit supporters (often nofollow — still brand) |
| **Podcasts / webinars** | Show notes link to your site |
| **Local business** | Chamber of commerce, local news, suppliers |

Community links are often **nofollow** but build brand and sometimes lead to editorial coverage.

## 8. Unlinked brand mentions

Monitor when sites mention your brand without linking:

| Tool / method | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Google Alerts | Brand name |
| Ahrefs / Semrush alerts | New mentions |
| `intext:"YourBrand" -site:yoursite.com` | Manual search |

**Email:** "Thanks for mentioning us in [article]. Would you mind adding a link to [canonical URL] so readers can find us?"

Keep it short; many editors will add the link.

## 9. What not to do

| Tactic | Problem |
|--------|---------|
| **Buying links** | Violates guidelines; sellers often on penalized networks |
| **Private blog networks (PBNs)** | Fake sites exist only for links |
| **Automated comment / forum spam** | Toxic profile |
| **Link exchanges at scale** | "You link me, I link you" rings look artificial |
| **Widget links** (unrelated embeds) | Old tactic; risky |
| **Article spinning** | Low-quality guest posts at volume |

If an agency promises "500 links in 30 days," walk away.

## 10. Campaign calendar (example)

| Month | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| 1 | Publish flagship guide + internal links |
| 2 | Resource page + broken link outreach |
| 3 | Guest post pitches (5–10 quality sites) |
| 4 | Small survey → digital PR push |
| Ongoing | Mention monitoring, update assets yearly |

Link building is **parallel** to content — not a one-time project.
