Outreach & templates
Great assets do not earn links by themselves — someone has to **know they exist**. Outreach is personalized email (or social DM) that makes it easy for an editor to say yes.

## 1. Outreach mindset

| Bad outreach | Good outreach |
|--------------|---------------|
| Mass blast, same template | Custom first line per prospect |
| "Please link to my site" | "This fixes your broken link / helps your readers" |
| Flattery without substance | Specific reference to their article |
| Hidden agenda (paid post disguised as editorial) | Transparent value exchange |

**Goal:** one helpful email, not ten follow-ups that feel like harassment.

## 2. Prospecting workflow

```text
Build list (50–100) → qualify (relevance + authority) → find contact
  → personalize → send → track → one polite follow-up → move on
```

| Field to track | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| Prospect URL | The page you want a link on |
| Contact name + email | Hunter.io, site contact, LinkedIn |
| Angle | Broken link / resource / guest post / mention |
| Status | Sent / replied / linked / declined |
| Date sent | Avoid duplicate pitches |

Keep prospects in a spreadsheet or lightweight CRM.

## 3. Finding the right contact

| Site size | Where to look |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Blog / niche site** | About page, author Twitter, `hello@` |
| **Publication** | Editor by vertical (use masthead) |
| **Company** | Marketing or partnerships — not `support@` for editorial |

Generic `info@` works for small sites; for larger ones, **wrong recipient = no response**.

## 4. Email structure

```text
Subject:  specific, short (reference their page or topic)
Line 1:   prove you read their content (one concrete detail)
Line 2:   the value you offer (fix, resource, guest topic)
Line 3:   your URL + why it fits
Close:    low-pressure CTA ("If useful, feel free to add it")
Signature: real name, role, site
```

**Length:** 80–120 words. Busy editors skim.

## 5. Template — broken link

```text
Subject: Broken link on [Page title]

Hi [Name],

I was reading your [topic] roundup at [URL] — helpful list.

The link to [broken domain/path] returns a 404. I maintain an updated
guide on the same topic: [your URL]. It covers [one specific gap].

No pressure — just thought it might be a useful swap if you're updating the page.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

## 6. Template — resource page

```text
Subject: Suggestion for your [topic] resources page

Hi [Name],

Your resources page at [URL] is one of the better curated lists on [topic].

I recently published [asset title] ([your URL]) — [one sentence on what it covers].
It might fit under your "[section name]" if you're still maintaining the list.

Either way, appreciate the work you put into the page.

Best,
[Name]
```

## 7. Template — guest post pitch

```text
Subject: Guest idea: [specific headline]

Hi [Name],

I've followed [Site] for a while — especially [specific article].

I'd like to pitch: "[Proposed title]" — aimed at [their audience pain point].
Outline: [3 bullet points, not a full draft in the pitch].

I can match your tone and won't promote unrelated products. Happy to adjust angle.

Interested?

[Name], [credentials one line]
```

Attach **writing samples** if they ask — not in the first email.

## 8. Template — unlinked mention

```text
Subject: Thanks for the mention in [article title]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for mentioning [Brand] in [URL] — glad the [product/feature] was useful.

Would you mind linking the mention to [canonical URL]? Makes it easier for readers to find us.

Appreciate it,
[Name]
```

## 9. Follow-up rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **One follow-up** | 5–7 business days later |
| **Shorter** | 2–3 sentences max |
| **No guilt** | "Bumping in case this got buried" — not "I never heard back" |
| **Stop** | After follow-up, mark declined and move on |

Persistent follow-ups on cold link requests burn reputation.

## 10. Response handling

| Reply | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Yes, will add** | Thank them; check live link in 1–2 weeks |
| **Send more info** | Short bullet facts, not a sales deck |
| **No** | Thank them; do not argue |
| **Pay for post** | Decline if it violates your ethics / guidelines |
| **Add nofollow only** | Accept — traffic and brand still count |

## 11. Scaling without spamming

| Scale safely | Avoid |
|--------------|-------|
| 10–20 personalized emails / week | 200 identical emails / day |
| Reuse **structure**, not identical copy | Same subject line to everyone |
| Batch by angle (broken links one week) | Mix angles randomly |
| Hire VA for **research**, you approve sends | Fully automated outreach bots |

Quality outreach is a **sales skill** applied to SEO.

## 12. Legal and disclosure

| Situation | Practice |
|-----------|----------|
| **Paid placement** | Use `rel="sponsored"`; disclose per FTC / local rules |
| **Affiliate links** | Disclose relationship |
| **Free product for review** | Disclose if material connection |

Undisclosed paid links risk **search penalties** and **regulatory** issues.
