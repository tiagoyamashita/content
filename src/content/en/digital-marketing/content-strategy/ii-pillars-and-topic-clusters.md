Pillars & topic clusters
A **pillar page** is a broad, authoritative hub on a core topic. **Cluster** articles cover subtopics in depth and link back — building **topical authority** for search and readers.

## 1. Hub-and-spoke model

```text
        ┌── spoke: keyword research
        │
PILLAR ─├── spoke: title tags
        │
        └── spoke: internal linking
```

| Role | Length / depth | Links |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Pillar** | Comprehensive (3k+ words or multi-section hub) | Links to all spokes |
| **Spoke** | Focused one sub-intent | Links to pillar + 1–2 related spokes |

## 2. Choosing pillar topics

Pick pillars that match **business priority** + **search demand**:

| Criteria | Question |
|----------|----------|
| ICP relevance | Does our buyer care? |
| Search potential | Do people query this cluster? |
| Differentiation | Can we say something unique? |
| Monetization path | Does it lead to signup/sale? |

Example pillar: "Complete guide to email marketing for SaaS" with spokes on deliverability, welcome series, segmentation.

## 3. Keyword cluster → URL map

| Subtopic (spoke) | Target query | URL |
|------------------|--------------|-----|
| Pillar | "email marketing saas" | `/guides/email-marketing-saas/` |
| Spoke A | "saas welcome email" | `/blog/welcome-email-series/` |
| Spoke B | "email deliverability spf" | `/blog/spf-dkim-setup/` |

Map in a spreadsheet before writing — avoids [cannibalization](../on-page-seo/iii-keywords-and-search-intent.md).

## 4. Internal linking rules for clusters

```text
Every spoke  →  links to pillar (contextual, above fold or early)
Pillar       →  links to every spoke (TOC or "in this guide" section)
Related spokes →  cross-link where genuinely related
```

Refresh pillar **quarterly** with new spokes and updated sections.

## 5. Topical authority (intuition)

Sites that cover a topic **completely and consistently** often rank new URLs in that topic faster than generalist sites.

| Signal | Practice |
|--------|----------|
| Depth | Multiple spokes, not one thin post |
| Freshness | Update pillar when tools/practices change |
| Expertise | Author bios, citations, original examples |

## 6. When not to cluster

| Situation | Instead |
|-----------|---------|
| One-off news | Single post, no forced pillar |
| Tiny total addressable content | One strong guide beats five weak spokes |
| YMYL without expertise | Partner with expert or narrow scope |
