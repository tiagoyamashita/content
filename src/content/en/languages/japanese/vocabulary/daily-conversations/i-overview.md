Daily conversations — overview
**Situational dialogues** for Japanese offices — with **keigo** marked and explained. Each file is a short script you can read aloud; annotations show **尊敬語** (elevating the other person), **謙譲語** (humbling yourself), and **丁寧語** (standard polite).

Before starting: skim [Keigo](../iii-keigo.md) for verb tables. For written mail, see [Email templates](../iv-email-templates.md). For one-line Slack replies, see [Verbs & office replies](../../day2day/i-beginner.md).

## How each dialogue is formatted

```text
Role — context (internal / client)

A: Japanese line
   (reading) — English gist
   [keigo: 謙譲語 いたします — humble "I will do"]
```

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **尊敬** | 尊敬語 — about their action |
| **謙譲** | 謙譲語 — about your action |
| **丁寧** | 丁寧語 — ます・です (minimum at work) |
