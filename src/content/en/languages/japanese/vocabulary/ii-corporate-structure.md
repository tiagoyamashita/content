Corporate Japan — org vocabulary
Titles, departments, employment types, and workplace process words you see in **org charts**, **email headers**, **meeting invites**, and **HR announcements**. Pair with [Common words](i-common-words.md) for IT delivery terms (要件定義書, 工数, …), [Keigo](iii-keigo.md) for client-facing honorifics, and [Verbs & office replies](../day2day/i-beginner.md) for polite verbs in email.

## How to use this page

1. Learn **titles** (部長, 課長) before guessing from English org charts — Japanese hierarchy labels do not map 1:1 to VP / Director / Manager.
2. Note **reading** for names on business cards and Slack display names.
3. SIer and vendor staff: focus on **IT / project roles** and **取引先・顧客** vocabulary.

## Title mapping (rough guide)

Japanese titles do not align cleanly with Western job grades. Use context — **company size**, **industry**, and whether the role is **line** or **project**.

| Japanese | Often closest Western label | Caveat |
|----------|----------------------------|--------|
| 部長 | Director / Head of … | May manage multiple 課; smaller firm = very senior |
| 課長 | Manager | Hands-on people manager in many firms |
| 係長 | Team lead / Supervisor | Closer to working lead than “manager” |
| 主任 | Senior specialist / lead | Not always people management |
| PL / PM | Project lead / PM | Project matrix, not always 課長 |
| 執行役員 | EVP / C-level without board seat | Common in listed companies |
